%% ===================== Abstract =====================
\begin{abstract}
Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) provide a flexible framework for
solving partial differential equations, yet their training loss does not
reliably reflect approximation error in Sobolev norms. In particular,
PINNs may exhibit small training loss while incurring substantial error
in the energy seminorm.

We derive a fully computable residual-based \emph{a posteriori} error estimator
for PINN approximations of coercive elliptic problems with soft Dirichlet
boundary enforcement. The estimator augments the classical residual term
with a harmonic lifting that accounts for boundary mismatch, yielding the bound
\[
\|\nabla(u - \hat{u})\|_{L^2(\Omega)}
\;\le\;
\frac{1}{\alpha}\|R\|_{H^{-1}(\Omega)}
+
\|\nabla w\|_{L^2(\Omega)}.
\]

We prove reliability in the energy seminorm for constant-coefficient problems.
For variable-coefficient diffusion, a Laplace-based lifting is used as a
computable surrogate for the exact $a$-harmonic lifting, resulting in a
practically reliable upper bound consistent with the computational framework.

A fully reproducible numerical study demonstrates effectivity indices
$\eta \in [1.05,\,1.31]$ across smooth, heterogeneous, and singular benchmarks,
including an L-shaped domain with a re-entrant corner. The results show that
boundary mismatch is the dominant source of error, while residual-only
estimators can underestimate the error by orders of magnitude.

These findings establish a principled and practical framework for
\emph{certifying} PINN solutions in the energy seminorm and highlight the
necessity of incorporating boundary contributions for reliable error estimation.
\end{abstract}
