\begin{abstract}
Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) provide flexible meshfree approximations for partial differential equations, but standard training losses do not certify the actual approximation error. We study a residual-based a posteriori estimator for coercive elliptic PINN solutions with soft Dirichlet boundary enforcement. The estimator combines the dual norm of the interior residual with a harmonic lifting term that captures the boundary mismatch induced by the penalty formulation. We implement a reproducible validation pipeline, including fitted-domain treatment for a singular L-shaped benchmark, and demonstrate reliable effectivities between $1.05$ and $1.31$ across smooth, heterogeneous, and singular test problems. The numerical study also confirms that residual-only estimates are insufficient: the lifting term is consistently the dominant contribution in the validated benchmarks.
\end{abstract}
