\subsection{Effectivity and ablation}

\Cref{fig:effectivity-benchmark} shows the benchmark-level effectivity indices. The sharpest performance is observed on the two unit-square problems, while the L-shaped singular benchmark shows a moderate but still acceptable loss of efficiency.

The ablation study in \Cref{fig:ablation-study} and \Cref{tab:ablation-results} isolates the contributions of the residual-only term, the lifting-only term, and the full estimator. The residual-only estimate is far below the full estimate in every benchmark. The lifting term dominates the estimate throughout the suite, confirming that soft boundary enforcement introduces a boundary-driven error component that must be included explicitly in any reliable PINN certification method.

\subsection{Interpretation}

Three conclusions follow from the validated numerical study:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item The full estimator is reliable and practically sharp across all supported coercive elliptic benchmarks.
\item Residual-only certification is inadequate: it systematically underrepresents the total error and misses the dominant boundary-driven contribution.
\item The singular L-shaped benchmark is more demanding than the smooth cases, but once geometry-consistent discretizations are used for the lifting, dual norm, and true-energy reference, the estimator remains both reliable and moderately efficient.
\end{enumerate}
